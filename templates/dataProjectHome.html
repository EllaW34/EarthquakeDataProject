About the Earthquakes


This is a website analysing data about 8,394 earthquakes that occured around the globe in July and August of 2016.

The data used is from a CORGIS Dataset Project that can be found here.

The dataset included details about each individual earthquake including the time and location that it occured. This data revealed that the majority of earthquakes happened in California and Alaska. The following pages will give more insight into the details of the specific locations among other factors.

{{info}}

About 20,000 earthquakes happen each year (55 every day) with 16 of them being major (greater than or equal to magnitude 7). The largest earthquake ever recorded was a 9.5 magnitude that stretched almost 1,000 miles across Chile on May 22nd in 1960.

Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates get stuck at their edges due to friction. When the stress overcomes the friction, there is a large release of energy that travels in waves and causes the shaking that we feel during an earthquake.
